Jeans are one of the most searched-for fashion items worldwide, with an average of 13 searches per second.

To compile this data deep dive into denim, global fashion search platform Lyst has tracked more than 60 million denim related queries over the last 12 months, crunching the queries, page views and sales data across 5 million fashion products from 12,000 designers and stores.

Searches for all things denim have increased 11% year on year, with shoppers looking for hundreds of different combinations of styles, colours, shapes and fits.

There is a growing interest in more eco-friendly denim with keywords such as “sustainable denim” and “organic cotton” becoming more and more popular amongst millennials. Over the past year, this has resulted in a 193% increase in page views for ethical denim brands such as Weekday, Re/done, Citizens of Humanity, and Nudie jeans. When searching for ethical fashion, sustainable denim is in the top 3 most used keywords.

On average, men are spending 31% more than women on a pair of jeans

While men are searching for jeans by premium denim brands, women tend to spend a bit less but buy more often, turning to more affordable retailers.

Maternity jeans sell out faster than any other denim item

Maternity jeans sell out faster than any other denim item, and Topshop, H&M and Asos have the world’s most wanted styles. The top 3 most searched maternity styles this year are boyfriend, black skinny, and jeggings.
